Project Overview

In Week 9, we designed the database structure for the NeuroStack Patient Management System. The goal was to build a scalable and organized relational database that supports patient management, doctor records, appointments, prescriptions, billing, and medical history.

Core Entities Designed

Patients

Stores patient details including personal and emergency contact information.

Doctors

Stores doctor details, specialization, and department information.

Appointments

Tracks patient-doctor appointments and visit scheduling.

Medical Records

Stores diagnosis, treatment notes, and medical history.

Prescriptions

Manages medication details, dosage, and prescription instructions.

Billing

Handles payment records, billing status, and transaction details.

Relationships Between Entities

  • Patient → Appointment (One-to-Many): A patient can book many appointments, and each appointment belongs to one patient.
  • Doctor → Appointment (One-to-Many): A doctor can attend many appointments, and each appointment is associated with one doctor.
  • Patient → Medical Records (One-to-Many): A patient can have multiple medical records over time.
  • Doctor → Medical Records (One-to-Many): A doctor can create multiple medical records.
  • Patient → Prescriptions (One-to-Many): A patient can receive multiple prescriptions.
  • Doctor → Prescriptions (One-to-Many): A doctor can issue multiple prescriptions.
  • Appointment → Billing (One-to-One): Each appointment generates exactly one billing record.
  • Patient → Billing (One-to-Many): A patient can have multiple billing records.

Assumptions & Constraints

  • Each patient and doctor has a unique ID.
  • Appointments must reference valid patient and doctor records.
  • Billing records must be tied to valid appointments.
  • Billing amounts must always be positive.
  • Appointment dates should follow business validation rules.

Future Enhancements

  • Add authentication tables for secure login integration.
  • Implement automated appointment reminders.
  • Store digital reports and uploaded medical documents.
  • Add analytics dashboard for hospital insights.

Key Learning

This deliverable helped us understand that a well-structured database is the foundation of any scalable software system. It improved our understanding of entity relationships, normalization, and real-world database planning.
